## Design Context

### Users
Health-conscious individuals (primarily 18-35) who want to understand and improve their skin health with scientific rigor. They open the app daily for a quick face scan, then review trends and product recommendations. They expect clinical accuracy wrapped in a premium consumer experience — like having a personal dermatologist in their pocket. Many are already invested in skincare routines and want data to validate or adjust their approach.

### Brand Personality
**Minimal. Scientific. Precise.**

The voice is confident but never clinical-cold — think a brilliant dermatologist who speaks plainly. Copy is concise, data-forward, and avoids filler. Numbers and visualizations do the talking. The brand earns trust through transparency (showing confidence levels, explaining methodology) rather than marketing language.

### Emotional Goals
1. **Calm confidence** — "I'm in good hands." The app feels authoritative and steady, never anxious or alarmist about skin conditions.
2. **Curiosity & delight** — "This is fascinating." Beautiful data visualizations and subtle animations make skin science feel like discovery, not diagnosis.
3. **Premium exclusivity** — "This feels expensive." Every interaction should feel polished and intentional, like a luxury health instrument.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Warm cream light mode (`#FAFAF7`) for daily screens, dark gradient (`#050a12 → #6B8799`) for scan/camera immersion. The duality reinforces the app's two modes: calm reflection (light) and focused analysis (dark).

**Color palette:**
- Primary teal `#3A9E8F` — clinical precision, health, clarity
- Secondary purple `#6366B5` — intelligence, data depth
- Signal colors are semantic: structure (teal), hydration (blue), inflammation (red), sun damage (amber), elasticity (purple)
- Glass morphism overlays for depth without heaviness
- Warm neutrals, never blue-gray — the app should feel organic, not sterile

**Typography:**
- App: Switzer (geometric sans-serif) — Regular for body, Bold for emphasis. No italic, no decorative weights.
- Landing: Bricolage Grotesque (display) + Plus Jakarta Sans (body)
- Hierarchy through size and weight, not color variation

**Motion:**
- Calm easing (`Easing.out(cubic)`) as the universal curve
- Staggered fade-rise entrances (120ms gaps) inspired by Headspace
- 6 motion tiers: fast (140ms) through breathe (2000ms)
- Motion should feel like breathing — never snappy or bouncy. Spring physics only for micro-interactions (press feedback).
- Every animation must serve comprehension or delight, never decoration.

**References:**
- **Oura Ring / Whoop** — data-rich health tracking, premium dark aesthetic, clear metric visualization, scientific credibility
- **Headspace / Calm** — warm organic illustrations, gentle staggered animations, calming color palette, progressive disclosure
- **Curology / Hims** — modern DTC skincare branding, approachable science communication, lifestyle integration

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Generic health app / Apple Health system-UI look — too cold, too utilitarian
- Overly playful / gamified aesthetic (badges exist but shouldn't dominate the visual language)
- Dense dashboards — prefer progressive disclosure, one insight at a time
- Blue-gray corporate palettes — stay warm and organic
- Aggressive conversion UI — paywalls should feel like invitations, not gates

### Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration** — Every pixel should communicate information or create breathing room. No ornamental elements. If a gradient, shadow, or animation doesn't serve comprehension or emotional tone, remove it.

2. **Calm authority** — The interface should feel like a steady hand, not an excited pitch. Use restraint in color, motion, and copy. Let data speak. Error states are calm and constructive, never alarming.

3. **Progressive revelation** — Show the essential insight first, then let users drill deeper. One question per onboarding screen. One key metric per card. Complexity exists but is layered, never dumped.

4. **Warmth in science** — Clinical accuracy doesn't mean clinical coldness. Warm cream backgrounds, organic teal accents, gentle animations, and human-readable labels bridge the gap between medical rigor and consumer comfort.

5. **Sensory polish** — Haptic feedback on key actions, smooth 60fps animations, glass morphism depth, and consistent spacing create a tactile premium feel. The app should feel as considered as the science behind it.

### Accessibility (WCAG AAA Target)
- Contrast ratios: 7:1 for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text and UI components
- Touch targets: minimum 48x48pt, recommended 56x56pt for primary actions
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — provide static alternatives for all animations
- Screen reader: comprehensive labels, logical focus order, announce dynamic content changes
- Color is never the sole indicator — always pair with icons, labels, or patterns
- Support Dynamic Type / system font scaling where feasible

### Component Conventions
- Cards: `borderRadius: 14-20`, soft shadow (`shadowOpacity: 0.08, radius: 20`), white or glass background
- Buttons: teal fill for primary, ghost/outline for secondary, full-width on mobile, `borderRadius: 999` (pill)
- Spacing: 8pt grid strictly. Padding inside cards: 16-24pt. Screen horizontal margins: 16pt.
- Icons: Feather icon set, 20px standard size, teal when active, muted gray when inactive
- Glass surfaces: `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.72-0.93)` with subtle border `rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.04-0.08)`
